Glossary of PK/PD Terms

Absorption — The process by which a drug moves from the site of administration into systemic circulation.

ADME — Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion; the four processes governing drug disposition.

Allometric scaling — Extrapolation of PK parameters across species or body sizes using power functions of body weight.

AUC (Area Under the Curve) — Total drug exposure over time; computed as the integral of the concentration-time profile.

Bioavailability (F) — The fraction of an administered dose that reaches systemic circulation unchanged.

BLOQ (Below Limit of Quantification) — A concentration below the assay’s lower limit of reliable measurement.

BSV (Between-Subject Variability) — Variability in PK parameters between different individuals.

Clearance (CL) — The volume of plasma completely cleared of drug per unit time (L/h).

Cmax — Maximum observed drug concentration after dosing.

Compartment — A mathematical abstraction representing a kinetically homogeneous pool of drug.

Covariate — A patient characteristic (e.g., weight, age, renal function) that explains variability in PK parameters.

Css — Steady-state concentration achieved when the rate of drug input equals the rate of elimination.

Distribution — The reversible transfer of drug between blood and tissues.

EC50 — Concentration producing 50% of the maximum drug effect.

Effect compartment — A hypothetical compartment linking plasma concentration to the site of drug action.

Elimination — Irreversible removal of drug from the body (metabolism + excretion).

Emax — Maximum drug-attributable effect.

Exposure-response — The relationship between a drug exposure metric and a clinical outcome.

First-order kinetics — A process where the rate is proportional to the amount/concentration present.

First-pass metabolism — Drug metabolism occurring before reaching systemic circulation (gut wall, liver).

Flip-flop kinetics — When the absorption rate constant is slower than the elimination rate constant.

Half-life (t_{1/2}) — Time required for drug concentration to decrease by 50%.

Hill coefficient (\gamma) — Parameter controlling the steepness of the sigmoid Emax curve.

Hysteresis — The phenomenon where concentration-effect relationship differs between rising and falling concentrations.

Indirect response model — A PD model where drug affects the production or loss of an endogenous mediator.

IVIVE — In Vitro to In Vivo Extrapolation; scaling in vitro data to predict in vivo PK.

ka — First-order absorption rate constant.

ke (k_{el}) — First-order elimination rate constant.

Km — Michaelis constant; concentration at which elimination rate is half of Vmax.

Kp — Tissue:plasma partition coefficient in PBPK models.

Michaelis–Menten kinetics — Saturable (capacity-limited) elimination described by Vmax and Km.

Micro-constants — Fundamental rate constants in a compartmental model (k_{10}, k_{12}, k_{21}).

Model-informed drug development (MIDD) — Use of quantitative models to inform drug development decisions.

NCA (Noncompartmental Analysis) — Estimation of PK parameters without assuming a specific compartmental model.

ODE (Ordinary Differential Equation) — Mathematical description of how quantities change over time.

PBPK (Physiologically Based Pharmacokinetics) — Mechanistic PK model incorporating anatomical and physiological compartments.

Pharmacodynamics (PD) — Study of the biochemical and physiological effects of drugs (what the drug does to the body).

Pharmacogenomics — Study of how genetic variation affects drug response.

Pharmacokinetics (PK) — Study of drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (what the body does to the drug).

Pharmacometrics — The science of quantitative pharmacology using mathematical and statistical models.

Potency — Amount of drug needed to produce a given effect; reflected by EC50 (lower = more potent).

PTA (Probability of Target Attainment) — The probability that a dosing regimen achieves a predefined PK/PD target.

RSE (Relative Standard Error) — Measure of parameter estimate precision; RSE% = (SE / estimate) × 100.

Shrinkage — The tendency of individual parameter estimates to “shrink” toward the population mean when data are sparse.

TDM (Therapeutic Drug Monitoring) — Individualization of dosing based on measured drug concentrations.

Tmax — Time at which Cmax occurs.

TMDD (Target-Mediated Drug Disposition) — Nonlinear PK due to saturable binding to the pharmacological target.

Vd (Volume of Distribution) — Apparent volume relating the amount of drug in the body to plasma concentration.

Vmax — Maximum rate of capacity-limited elimination.

VPC (Visual Predictive Check) — Simulation-based diagnostic comparing observed data to model predictions.

Zero-order kinetics — A process where the rate is constant and independent of concentration.
